CENTRO ABOUT US

Who We Are

Established in 1977, CENTRO, formerly Centro Las Americas, is the largest minority-led, community-based, multiservice, multicultural, multilinguistic, nonprofit organization in Central Massachusetts, serving over 24,000 people each year. CENTRO’s primary purpose is to assist individuals and families striving to reach self-sufficiency while promoting social responsibility, fostering cultural identity, and encouraging families to be significant contributors to the community. CENTRO is a CARF-accredited institution.

We Promise To

  • Provide culturally and linguistically competent services that help clients striving to be self-sufficient achieve their dreams.
  • Be compassionate and treat our clients with respect.
  • Carry ourselves with dignity, integrity, and strive for excellence.
  • Not make excuses or take shortcuts, and ensure our work positively impacts clients.
  • Support co-workers and provide value to stakeholders.
  • Help make CENTRO the best place to work in all of Central MA.

Our Pledge

We achieve our mission by providing culturally and linguistically competent case management, community-based social, behavioral, mental health, and health-related services while preserving and sharing our diverse cultural values and experiences. This is possible by establishing a strong bond with our clients and the communities we serve and leveraging partnerships and collaborations with other providers, colleges, universities, hospitals, government entities, and other institutions.

Our Strategic Planning Process

Our strategic planning process for Vision 2020 is in its second year of implementation. The plan, developed over the last six months of FY 2019, involved input from 52 community stakeholders, including board members, staff, clients, and other representatives. Feedback was gathered through surveys, employee retreats, and a strategic analysis by a Clark University graduate group.

Our Programs and Services

CENTRO serves over 8,700 families across 52 cities and towns in MA, providing a range of services through three divisions: Family Services, One Health, and New Americans Community Development Corporation. The strategic plan for 2020 outlines key goals, including strengthening the workforce, enhancing clinical care, expanding cultural competence, and ensuring long-term financial sustainability.
